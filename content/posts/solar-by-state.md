How much electricity a rooftop solar system produces depends heavily on where you live. The table below compares the estimated annual output of a standard **6 kW residential system** across US states, ranked from highest to lowest production. Peak sun hours represent the average number of hours per day that sunlight is strong enough to generate at full rated capacity. Sunnier states like Arizona and Nevada produce noticeably more per panel than cloudier northern states, but solar can still be cost-effective in lower-sun states where electricity rates are high. The savings estimate uses a national average rate and is illustrative only; your actual savings depend on your local utility rate and net-metering policy.
